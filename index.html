Dept. of Computer Science & Engineering

ahmaungoo24@gmail.com

tarannumbloch@marwadiuniversity.ac.in
Abstract

Recognition of handwritten digits in Burmese script remains underexplored in the OCR literature, particularly under the constraint of inexpensive hardware. Existing work on the Burmese Handwritten Digit Dataset (BHDD) optimizes solely for classification accuracy, neglecting model size and computational cost. We present MyanNet, a compact CNN combining depthwise separable convolutions, global average pooling (GAP), and batch normalization, designed to approach state-of-the-art accuracy within a parameter budget suitable for mobile and embedded deployment. MyanNet achieves 99.49% accuracy on the BHDD test set with only 10,634 trainable parameters— a 69.5% reduction over a standard CNN baseline at a cost of 0.09 percentage points. A 30-trial Optuna search identifies optimal hyperparameters; 5-fold cross-validation confirms stability (99.46% ± 0.06%). After TFLite integer quantization, the model occupies 24.18 KB and runs inference in 0.263 ms on CPU. To our knowledge, this is the first BHDD study to treat edge deployability as a primary design requirement.
